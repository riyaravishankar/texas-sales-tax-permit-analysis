# Texas Sales Tax Permit Analysis

## Overview

This project examines how COVID-19 case growth influenced new business formation across Texas counties during the first year of the pandemic.

Using county-level COVID-19 case data and Texas sales tax permit records, the analysis investigates whether counties experiencing severe increases in COVID-19 cases also experienced larger declines in new business activity.

The project was developed in Tableau as part of a graduate-level Data Visualization course at The University of Texas at Dallas.

---

## Business Question

Did Texas counties with extreme COVID-19 case growth experience larger declines in new business formation than counties with lower case growth?

---

## Hypothesis

Between March-May and June-August 2020, Texas counties with more than 2,000% COVID-19 case growth experienced a significantly larger decline in newly issued sales tax permits than counties with lower COVID-19 growth.

---

## Key Findings

- Counties with ≥2,000% COVID-19 growth experienced an average **20% decline** in newly issued sales tax permits.
- Counties with <2,000% COVID-19 growth experienced an average **7% decline**.
- High-growth counties showed stagnant business formation throughout the study period.
- Geographic clustering of high-impact counties was observed across parts of Texas.
- A negative relationship between COVID-19 growth and permit issuance was visible across counties.

These findings suggest that severe public health disruptions were associated with reduced entrepreneurial activity and slower business formation.
